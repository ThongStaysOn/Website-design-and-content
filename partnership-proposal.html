Kekspace × Ink

Partnership Proposal

Kekspace turns dApps into places Kraken has the rails; Kekspace gives those rails a social interface.

The Short Version

Kekspace is a live browser-based social world for Web3. For Ink, it turns dApps into places. Instead of asking users to jump between fragmented tabs, dashboards and chat apps, Kekspace lets them bridge in, verify, trade, lend, discover communities, display onchain identity, and coordinate around projects — inside a lightweight browser world that is already live in public beta.

Not a concept

We are not applying with a concept. We are applying with a live product and an Ink-native expansion plan. Our go-to-market is simple: take Ink's products to market.

What is Kekspace?

Kekspace is a social interface for Web3 that blends the social elements of early 2000s MMOs with real onchain utility. Think of the virtual worlds people grew up in — Habbo Hotel, Club Penguin, early Second Life — rebuilt with real digital ownership on the blockchain.

Players hang out in a sprawling isometric pixel city. They customize characters from head to toe, play arcade games, earn tickets, complete quests, decorate private rooms, collect NFTs, and trade with each other. The world runs seasonal events, hosts community gatherings, and keeps growing.

Live product

It is not a metaverse pitch deck. It is a live game with people in it.

What can you do in Kekspace?

  • Play games — Survivor (wave-based shooter), Frog Bowling, Whack-A-Meme, with Pokek (poker with onchain settlement on Ink) and Fishing coming soon
  • Customize your character — thousands of combinations across 15+ equipment slots: hats, hair, shirts, pants, shoes, glasses, pets, backpacks, and more
  • Decorate your own room — grid-based furniture system with seasonal items
  • Complete quests — NPCs throughout the world offer missions, with a ranking and badge system tracking progression
  • Collect and trade — skins, furniture, and equipment designed to be ownable onchain as NFTs through Ink
  • Hang out — chat, emotes, community events, and a world that is always active

Who is it for?

Gamers, communities, creators, and anyone who thinks virtual worlds should be fun again. Kekspace attracts crypto-native users who are already comfortable with wallets — the exact audience Ink wants to onboard.

Integration Modules

Seven modules define the Kekspace deployment on Ink. Each one maps directly to real Ink infrastructure and existing Ink applications.

🛬
Module 1

Ink Arrival District

The front door to Ink — bridge, verify, quest, go.

A dedicated first-stop area where users arrive in guest mode or connected mode, connect a wallet or embedded smart account, bridge into Ink, verify their identity, receive a first quest, and get routed into their first protocol action. Relay expresses cross-chain movement naturally in a visual interface. InkScore already rewards Bridge IN, making the arrival flow seamless.

  • Guest mode world entry and wallet connect
  • Optional smart-account / AA-based onboarding
  • Visualized bridge journey with live status updates
  • Kraken Verify "passport office"
  • First quest routing to Nado, Tydro and other rooms
  • Analytics for connect-to-bridge-to-first-action conversion
📈
Module 2

Nado Trading Arcade

Social trading rooms with installable terminals and builder-code distribution.

A social trading layer built around Nado terminals inside Kekspace. Official Nado areas in the Ink district, plus installable terminals that users, communities and room owners can place in their own rooms. Rooms become mini brokerages, communities become trading clubs, and social spaces become distribution surfaces. Nado's Builder Codes let Kekspace route and monetize activity natively.

  • Nado trading booths in official Ink areas
  • Installable terminals inside player-made rooms
  • Room-specific builder-code attribution
  • Live market boards and tickers
  • Trader leaderboards
  • Future social/copy-trading mechanics
🏦
Module 3

Tydro Bank

The bank of the city — lending made legible.

A dedicated banking district that turns lending and borrowing into something visually understandable. Tydro is one of the most important financial apps on Ink. Kekspace makes it easier for ordinary users to understand and trust — useful for deposit growth, borrower activation, capital retention, and ecosystem education.

  • Deposit desk and supply vault
  • Borrow desk with collateral visualizer
  • Health factor meter and risk warnings
  • "What happens if price moves?" explainer UI
  • Guided quests for first-time lenders and borrowers
  • Optional community vault rooms
💰
Module 4

Velodrome Liquidity Bazaar

AMM education, pool discovery, and LP recruitment.

A market-style room where users learn how AMMs work, discover pools, and LP into them through a more visual interface. For Ink's Superchain liquidity story, Kekspace makes pool discovery easier, helps users understand stable vs volatile pool logic, and makes LP actions feel less abstract.

  • Liquidity discovery boards
  • Stable vs volatile pool explainers
  • Pool explorer and search
  • Deposit LP flow and fee claiming
  • Protocol booths for LP recruitment
  • Visual liquidity map of key ecosystem pairs
🏅
Module 5

InkScore + Kraken Verify Social Layer

Onchain activity becomes socially useful.

A visible reputation layer that pulls onchain status into the social fabric of the game. InkScore gives visible progression based on onchain activity. Kraken Verify gives wallets a reusable verified status without exposing personal data. Kekspace turns those into actual social UX — badges, gating, cosmetics, and chat signals.

  • Verified badges and flairs
  • Room gating by Verify status or InkScore
  • Cosmetics and furniture unlocks tied to real activity
  • Chat signals, emojis and nameplates
  • Asset-holder and hold-duration badges (opt-in)
  • Privacy controls for wallet-derived signals
🏠
Module 6

Project / Community HQ Rooms

Where protocols live — chatrooms, websites, and event venues in one.

Permanent branded rooms that projects and communities use as chatrooms, websites, discovery surfaces, event venues and protocol front doors. For Ink apps, these become branded protocol spaces, help desks, onboarding zones, trading clubs, treasury and governance spaces, and event rooms for launches, AMAs and campaigns.

  • Branded room templates with plugin slots
  • Embedded protocol terminals (Nado, Tydro, bridge kiosks)
  • Token / Verify / score gating
  • Event scheduling and host controls
  • Project announcements and onboarding quests
  • Social hangout space with utility attached
🎈
Module 7 — Breakout Upside

Drop Party Launchpad

A game-native, nostalgic, fair-launch attention engine.

A launch mechanism inspired by Runescape-style drop parties. A founder configures the launch at a chest. Users gather in the room. Balloons drop in timed waves. Players move to pop them. Popping grants a chance at an allocation from the communal launch pool. The event continues until the bonding mechanism or fill condition is complete. Liquidity migrates or routes to the next venue.

The Drop Party is not just a meme idea. It is a potential distribution engine combining speculation, nostalgia, visual spectacle, live participation, social ceremony, and a fairness story. The mechanism can reduce sniping, bundling, pure speed advantage, and cabal-style early concentration through per-wallet wave limits, random drop positions and timing, Verify-gated rooms, cooldowns, and VRF-backed event randomness.

Framed as a phase 2 / co-design milestone alongside the core deployment — the viral flagship that can generate outsized attention and cultural distribution for Ink.

The Product — Proof It Works

The Krakening

We do not need to speculate about whether brand integration works inside Kekspace. We already did it. The Krakening was a live event in partnership with Kraken where the brand was embedded natively into the game world — themed outfits, environmental lighting, event branding, and custom quests.

  • Hundreds of players gathered in real-time for a midnight event
  • Exclusive limited-edition collectibles were distributed — and they are still being traded
  • A "ticket rain" mechanic rewarded players for being present and engaged
  • The event generated thousands of in-game interactions and social media posts
  • Community engagement spiked across Discord, Telegram, and Twitter
Not a banner ad

Not a banner ad. A living brand experience inside a living world.

Seasonal Events

The world transforms with the seasons. Halloween turns the entire world dark with a maze, cursed candies, themed quests and exclusive costumes. Christmas covers every zone in snow with an advent calendar, daily gifts and exclusive seasonal items. On Ink, each event becomes a predictable, repeatable burst of onchain activity — minting spikes, trading surges, and social visibility.

The Economy

Not just a game with a token attached. The entire economy is designed around a play-to-value loop: Explore, Gather, Craft, Equip or Sell, Mint. Deployed on Ink, every character customization becomes a potential NFT mint. Every furniture purchase becomes an onchain transaction. This is the opportunity: TVL, transaction volume, and daily active wallets.

The Billboard Network

In-game billboard network across high-traffic zones. Players interact with billboards to view expanded content, access external links, or browse NFT collections. Ink-native projects can advertise inside Kekspace to reach engaged, crypto-native users. A marketing platform for Ink, not just a game on Ink.

Epic Games Store

Positioning for distribution through the Epic Games Store — 78 million monthly active users, free to play, every transaction settling on Ink. The Web2-to-Web3 bridge that every L2 wants. Millions of gamers exposed to an Ink-native game where onchain ownership is baked into the experience.
