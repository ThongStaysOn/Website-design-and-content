# Kekspace Pitch Deck — Strategy & Context

## Who this is for

**Kraken** — specifically their **INK L2** team. This deck pitches Kekspace as a flagship project for the INK ecosystem.

## The angle

Kekspace isn't pitching cold. The Krakening event already proved the partnership works — hundreds of players showed up in real-time, exclusive collectibles are still being traded, and the brand integration felt native. This deck says: **"We already did this together once. Here's the full picture of what Kekspace is and why it belongs on INK."**

## Key messages per section

### Cover (Page 01)
- Kekspace + INK branding together
- Position as a partnership, not a cold pitch

### Welcome (Page 02)
- Quick intro: what Kekspace is, how long it's been around
- Establish credibility: community-mined since 2016, no presale, real history
- Signal: this isn't a new project looking for a chain — this is an established community looking for the right home

### About the Game (Page 03)
- What it is, what you can do, who it's for
- Frame it through INK's lens: this brings real users, real engagement, and real on-chain activity to the L2

### Economy & Blockchain (Page 04)
- Show the wallet integration and on-chain mechanics
- **Key message for Kraken:** every player action generates on-chain transactions on INK — NFT mints, item trades, currency transfers. This is TVL and transaction volume, not just a game.

### NFTs (Page 05)
- Show the NFT ecosystem: gallery, minting, collecting
- **Key message:** all of this mints on INK. Every piece of art, every collectible, every item = on-chain activity on their L2.

### Play to Value (Page 06)
- The economic loop: Explore → Gather → Craft → Equip/Sell → Mint
- **Key message:** this loop creates sustained on-chain engagement, not one-time transactions. Players keep coming back because progression has real value.

### Skins & Cosmetics (Page 07)
- Character customization with on-chain ownership
- **Key message:** cosmetic NFTs are the highest-volume, lowest-friction entry point for new users into the INK ecosystem.

### City Overview (Page 08)
- Show the scale of the world
- **Key message:** this isn't a single game — it's a platform. Multiple zones, multiple activities, growing constantly.

### Zones (Pages 09-12)
- Showcase variety: Spawn, Amplexus, Arcade, Cemetery
- **Key message:** every zone is a potential brand integration opportunity (just like The Krakening proved).

### Characters & Anatomy (Page 13)
- Deep customization system
- **Key message:** character investment = player retention. Players who build an identity stick around.

### Minigames (Page 14)
- Multiple game modes
- **Key message:** each minigame is a separate engagement loop. More games = more reasons to return = more on-chain activity.

### Pokek Poker (Page 15)
- On-chain poker
- **Key message:** this is DeFi-adjacent gaming. Real stakes, real pots, real on-chain settlement. High-value transactions on INK.

### Quests & Badges (Page 16)
- Progression system
- **Key message:** quests create daily active users. Badges create status. Both drive retention and repeat transactions.

### The Krakening (Pages 17-18)
- **This is the proof section.** The partnership already happened. Show the numbers.
- Hundreds of players in real-time
- Exclusive collectibles still being traded
- Brand integration felt native, not forced
- **Key message:** "We already proved this works. Now imagine it on INK with deeper integration."

### Halloween Event (Page 19)
- Seasonal world transformation
- **Key message:** Kekspace runs live events that drive spikes in engagement. This is the kind of content that brings users to INK during key moments.

### Community (Page 20)
- Real screenshots of real community gatherings
- **Key message:** this isn't a ghost chain project. These are real, active users who show up.

### Billboard Network (Page 21)
- In-game advertising infrastructure
- **Key message for Kraken:** the billboard network is a revenue layer AND a marketing channel. INK ecosystem projects could advertise inside Kekspace to reach engaged crypto-native users.

### Kekpads (Page 22)
- Player-owned rooms
- **Key message:** virtual real estate on INK. Each room is an on-chain asset. Ownership, customization, and social utility.

### Furniture & Rooms (Page 23)
- Room decoration system
- **Key message:** furniture items are NFTs. Every decoration purchase = an on-chain transaction on INK.

### Creator API (Page 24)
- Community-made minigames
- **Key message:** this turns Kekspace into a platform, not just a game. Third-party developers building on top = exponential growth of on-chain activity on INK.

### Epic Games Store (Page 25)
- Distribution through Epic
- **Key message:** 70M monthly active users exposed to an INK-native game. This is the bridge from Web2 to INK.

### Back Cover (Page 26)
- CTA: Let's build this on INK
- Contact info, social links

## Suggested additional slides

These pages don't exist yet but would strengthen the Kraken/INK pitch:

### NEW: INK Integration Overview
- **What:** A page showing how Kekspace integrates with INK specifically — wallet connection, transaction flow, smart contract deployment
- **Why:** Shows Kraken this isn't theoretical. Show the technical alignment.
- **Position in deck:** After page 04 (Economy & Blockchain)

### NEW: The Krakening — By the Numbers
- **What:** A stats page for The Krakening event with hard numbers — player count, concurrent users, collectibles minted, social impressions, items still in circulation
- **Why:** Numbers are more convincing than screenshots. This is the proof page.
- **Position in deck:** After page 18 (Krakening Exclusives)

### NEW: On-Chain Activity Metrics
- **What:** Transaction volume, NFTs minted, wallets connected, daily active users — whatever real data exists
- **Why:** Kraken cares about TVL and chain activity. Show them what Kekspace brings to INK in concrete terms.
- **Position in deck:** After page 06 (Play to Value)

### NEW: Brand Integration Opportunities
- **What:** A page showing all the ways brands can integrate into Kekspace — billboards, events, exclusive collectibles, zone sponsorships, in-game items
- **Why:** Positions Kekspace as a marketing platform for INK ecosystem projects, not just a game.
- **Position in deck:** After page 21 (Billboard Network)

### NEW: Roadmap — What INK Enables
- **What:** Features that become possible specifically because of INK — lower gas fees for microtransactions, faster minting, cross-game item interoperability
- **Why:** Shows Kraken that Kekspace isn't just porting to INK — INK unlocks things that weren't possible before.
- **Position in deck:** After page 24 (Creator API), before Epic Games Store

### NEW: The Ask
- **What:** A clear page stating what Kekspace wants from the partnership — grants, co-marketing, technical support, ecosystem placement, etc.
- **Why:** Every pitch deck needs a clear ask. What do you want them to do after reading this?
- **Position in deck:** Page 25, right before the back cover

### NEW: Team / About Meme Time Studios
- **What:** Brief intro to the team behind Kekspace
- **Why:** Kraken wants to know who they're partnering with. Even 1 page with names/roles builds trust.
- **Position in deck:** After The Ask, before back cover
